## Section-by-Section Strategy
 
### English
- First move: read the full sentence before looking at answer choices
- Skip rule: if you've read an answer twice and still unsure, pick your best and move on
- Rhetoric questions: STOP strategy — Summarize, Tone, Order, Purpose
- Watch for: redundancy, comma splices, dangling modifiers, wrong transition logic
- Time check at Q38: should be at ~23 min remaining
### Math
- First move: read the full question, identify what is actually being asked
- Write every step — no mental math on problems 40+
- Skip rule: no progress in 90 seconds → mark and move on
- Problems 1–30: should take 25 min (50 sec avg)
- Problems 31–60: remaining 35 min (70 sec avg — you'll need it)
- Time check at Q30: should be at ~35 min remaining
### Reading
- First move: note the passage type, choose your order
- My optimal passage order: [fill in after Week 3]
- 8 min per passage hard cap — if at 7 min and not done, guess remaining, move on
- For inference questions: find the evidence line before choosing
- Time check after passage 2: should be at ~17 min remaining
### Science
- First move: scan figure titles and axis labels before reading passage text
- Conflicting viewpoints: read ALL viewpoints completely before any questions
- If you can't answer from the figure: find the one relevant sentence in the passage
- Science never requires outside knowledge — the answer is always in the passage or figure
- Time check after passage 3: should be at ~14 min remaining

## If You Fall Behind on Time
 
- **English:** Skip the hardest rhetoric questions, come back. Grammar rules are faster.
- **Math:** Hard problems 50–60 are worth the same as easy problems 1–10. Don't over-invest.
- **Reading:** Guess on your weakest passage type questions. Protect your strongest type.
- **Science:** Conflicting viewpoints takes longest — do it last if behind.
