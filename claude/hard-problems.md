# Math — Hard Problems Strategy (Problems 50–60 Level)
 
*The goal is eliminating the 2–3 hard problems missed per test to push your Math score to the top range.*
 
---
 
## High-Frequency Hard Problem Types
 
### Advanced Trigonometry
- **Unit circle:** Know all 16 standard angles in radians and degrees, with exact sin/cos/tan values
- **Trig identities:** sin²x + cos²x = 1; tan x = sin x / cos x; double angle formulas
- **Law of sines:** a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C
- **Law of cosines:** c² = a² + b² – 2ab cos C
- **Inverse trig:** arcsin, arccos, arctan — know their domains and ranges
### Logarithms
- log_b(x) = y means b^y = x
- log(ab) = log a + log b
- log(a/b) = log a – log b
- log(a^n) = n log a
- Natural log: ln(e^x) = x; e^(ln x) = x
- Common trap: log(a + b) ≠ log a + log b
### Complex Numbers
- i = √(–1); i² = –1; i³ = –i; i⁴ = 1 (cycle repeats)
- Multiply: (a + bi)(c + di) = ac + adi + bci + bdi² = (ac – bd) + (ad + bc)i
- Divide: multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate
- Absolute value: |a + bi| = √(a² + b²)
### Matrices (rare but appears at 35–36 level)
- 2×2 multiplication: row × column
- Determinant: ad – bc
- Know when matrix multiplication is defined (columns of first = rows of second)
### 3D Geometry
- Volume of sphere: (4/3)πr³
- Surface area of sphere: 4πr²
- Volume of cone: (1/3)πr²h
- Distance in 3D: √((x₂–x₁)² + (y₂–y₁)² + (z₂–z₁)²)
- Diagonal of a rectangular box: √(l² + w² + h²)
### Statistics & Probability
- Mean, median, mode — know what changes each when values are added/removed
- Standard deviation: larger spread = larger SD
- Expected value = Σ(value × probability)
- Probability of A AND B (independent) = P(A) × P(B)
- Probability of A OR B = P(A) + P(B) – P(A AND B)
- Combinations: C(n,r) = n! / (r!(n–r)!)
- Permutations: P(n,r) = n! / (n–r)!
### Advanced Coordinate Geometry
- Circle equation: (x – h)² + (y – k)² = r²
- Complete the square to find center and radius
- Parabola: y = a(x – h)² + k (vertex form)
- Distance from point to line: |ax₁ + by₁ + c| / √(a² + b²)

## The Skip-and-Return Rule
 
Problems 50–60 are designed to take 2+ minutes each. On a 60-minute test, spending 4 minutes on problem 55 can cost you problems 56–60 entirely.
 
**Rule:** If you haven't made progress in 90 seconds, mark the problem and move on. Return in the final 10 minutes.
 
**What counts as progress:** writing a formula, drawing a diagram, eliminating 1 answer choice
 
---
 
## Careless Error Checklist
 
After every Math session, ask for each wrong answer:
- [ ] Did I read the question fully? (What are they actually asking for?)
- [ ] Did I use the right formula?
- [ ] Did I make an arithmetic error?
- [ ] Did I solve for the wrong variable?
- [ ] Did I misread a sign (+/–)?
- [ ] Did I forget to square or take the square root?
If most errors are from this list → it's a careless problem, not a concept problem. The fix is slowing down on the final step.

## Problem 60 Patterns
 
The final few Math questions almost always involve:
1. A graph or figure that requires interpreting a function
2. A word problem with multiple steps (set up an equation, then solve)
3. A combined concepts problem (trig + geometry, or algebra + statistics)
4. A "what must be true" or "what could be true" logic question
**Strategy:** Write out every step. Don't do multi-step problems in your head at this difficulty level.
